With our $190B backlog dependent on these capabilities, we have 90 days to build workforce resilience before market AI adoption accelerates beyond our control.

**THE CRITICAL RISK: WHERE SAFETY MEETS AUTOMATION**

Defense & Systems and Manufacturing & Supply Chain represent GE Aerospace's highest-risk segments, with 1,485 employees sitting at the dangerous intersection of high automation vulnerability and safety-critical operations. Defense & Systems shows 37.2% automation risk across 97.1% safety-critical roles, while Manufacturing & Supply Chain shows 53.4% automation risk across 96.6% safety-critical operations. This violates our fundamental SQDC hierarchy—we cannot optimize for cost and delivery improvements through AI while compromising safety and quality in segments that represent the operational backbone of our $190B backlog delivery capability.

**THREE STRATEGIC FINDINGS**

**Finding 1:** 2,193 employees show FLIGHT DECK gaps exceeding 70 points while sitting in automation-vulnerable roles—these are our people least equipped for AI augmentation who need it most for backlog delivery, creating a continuous improvement paradox that threatens our operational discipline.

**Finding 2:** Manufacturing & Supply Chain's AI readiness score of 38.6 represents our lowest segment capability, yet this segment faces 53.4% automation pressure—without immediate intervention, supply chain disruption will cascade through our entire $190B backlog delivery timeline.

**Finding 3:** Only 9.2% of roles achieve Judgment-Heavy classification, but these 4,607 employees represent our irreplaceable capability core—respect for people demands we identify and develop more employees into this category before industry talent loss accelerates.

**WHAT THE SHAP MODEL FOUND**

The AI vulnerability of individual employees—not their job function—drives automation risk by a factor of four over any other characteristic. This means workforce transformation is fundamentally about people's relationship with change, not organizational redesign. The model revealed that aerospace regulatory compliance expertise is the strongest predictor of irreplaceable roles, while employees practicing FLIGHT DECK disciplines most deeply—kaizen, A3 problem solving, daily visual management—consistently land in judgment-heavy positions that hold our operating system together under pressure.
